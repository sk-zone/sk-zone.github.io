About Me

My research interests sit at the crossroads of trustworthy machine learning and computational biology, where I develop multimodal models for healthcare challenges that affect populations at scale, with a particular focus on prevention and prognosis for infectious diseases and viral pathologies.

I focus on multimodal fusion and causal approaches that integrate pathology imaging, clinical time-series, and epidemiological data to identify predictive biomarkers, remain robust under adversarial conditions, and generate explainable insights that are actionable for clinicians and public health.

My prior work includes publications on adversarial robustness, algorithmic fairness, and explainability in socio-technical contexts.

I am always open to discussions and collaborations on avant-garde research directions that push the boundaries of both science and care.
